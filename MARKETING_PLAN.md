# OneShetland — Launch Marketing Plan

*Everything Shetland, in one place — built for the islands, by the islands.*

This plan is built around two facts: **what OneShetland is** (a multi‑section community super‑app) and **how Shetland actually works** (a small, tight, high‑trust island community of ~23,000 where word of mouth, Facebook and local media reach almost everyone). The strategy leans into both.

---

## 1. The Shetland reality (this shapes everything)

- **Tiny but dense.** ~22,900 people, ~11,000 households; Lerwick ~6,800, the rest spread across Mainland and the outlying isles (Yell, Unst, Whalsay, Bressay, Burra, Scalloway, Brae…). You don't need "scale" — you need **penetration and depth**. Reaching most households is genuinely achievable.
- **Trust is everything; "kent folk" carry the message.** Slick, outside‑feeling marketing is viewed warily. Local voices, transparency and visible community benefit do the heavy lifting. Lead with *Shetland's* benefit, not the company.
- **Facebook is the town square.** Community council pages, buy‑and‑sell groups, "Shetland" interest groups. Critically — **most local businesses run on Facebook and have no website** (we saw this directly: of 134 listed businesses, only ~2 had a site). That's a gap OneShetland fills, *and* FB groups are the distribution channel.
- **Local media reaches nearly everyone:** *The Shetland Times* (weekly paper, near‑universal readership), *Shetland News* (online), *BBC Radio Shetland*, *SIBC*. High trust, low cost (earned, not paid).
- **Life runs on a seasonal calendar:** Up Helly Aa season (Jan–Mar, Lerwick + rural fire festivals), Shetland Folk Festival (spring), agricultural shows & regattas (summer — Cunningsburgh, Walls, Voe, Burra Regatta), Shetland Wool Week (autumn, global knitters), cruise‑ship summer footfall in Lerwick, Christmas shop‑local. **Time the launch to a moment.**
- **Cruise tourism is a summer force of its own.** Around **150 cruise calls** bring **~140,000 passengers** to Lerwick each season (roughly May–Oct). On a peak day that's up to **~4,800 people ashore** — close to Lerwick's own population (~6,800). That's a major footfall opportunity for local businesses *and* a real "is town going to be mobbed today?" question for residents. OneShetland's **Cruise** section answers both, from the same dataset.
- **The Shetland world is bigger than the islands.** A large diaspora and a global community with Shetland heritage/affinity (Hamefarin, Wool Week knitters, fishing‑heritage followers). The web + Spik/Da Boats/Memories/What's On reach them — a growth lane beyond the 23k.

---

## 2. Positioning & messaging

**Positioning:** the one warm home for everything Shetland — what's on, local businesses, the fleet, the dialect, community groups, jobs and more — owned and run for the islands, keeping money and life local.

**Message pillars**
1. **All of Shetland, one place, one account.** Stop hopping between Facebook pages, posters and group chats.
2. **Built for the islands, by the islands.** Local, warm, dialect‑flavoured (Spik). Not corporate.
3. **Community‑first, not extractive.** Tiny fees (95p, not grabby percentages), donations & Gift Aid for hubs, a local wallet that keeps spend in Shetland. *We win when Shetland wins.*
4. **Gives every business a proper home** beyond a Facebook page — for free to claim.

**Tone:** warm, plain‑spoken, proud, a little playful. Dialect used with affection (Spik). The painterly woven‑rings brand = "many strands, one Shetland."

---

## 3. Core strategy — "many front doors, one home"

Don't market "an app." Market **each section to its own tribe through that tribe's natural channel** — every door opens into the same app with one account, and density compounds (a knitter arrives for Wool Week events, stays for the directory, the Spik word and Da Boats).

| Section | Who it's for | The hook | Where to reach them | First KPI |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **What's On** (tickets) | Everyone; event‑goers & organisers | "Every Shetland event in one place — and sell tickets with 95p fees" | Press, FB events, venues (Mareel, halls), organisers | Events listed; tickets sold |
| **Local** (directory) | Shoppers + 130+ businesses | "We've already built your page — come **claim** it, free" | Door‑to‑door on Commercial St, Living Lerwick BID, FB | Businesses claimed |
| **Fetch** (delivery) | Households + drivers | "Local delivery, run by local folk" | Driver recruitment first; then households | Drivers live; first deliveries |
| **Hubs** (clubs/charities) | Community groups & their members | "Memberships, notices & donations (with Gift Aid) — free to run" | Direct to clubs/charities; they bring members | Hubs onboarded; members joined |
| **Jobs & Shifts** | Workers + employers | "Shetland work, from full‑time to a single shift" | Employers, SIC, FB jobs groups | Jobs posted; applications |
| **Da Boats** | Fishing families, enthusiasts, diaspora | "Track da boats, live" | Whalsay/Burra/Scalloway communities, FB, diaspora | Saved vessels; shares |
| **Spik** | Cultural pride + diaspora | "Today's Shetland wird" — daily, shareable | FB, Instagram, press, schools | Daily opens; shares |
| **Memories** | Heritage & nostalgia | "Pin your Shetland memory on the map" | Shetland Museum & Archives, FB heritage groups | Memories posted |
| **Games** | Daily habit / light viral | "Guess da Wird / Spik Sprint" | In‑app + social streaks | Daily players; streaks |
| **Cruise** | Locals, Lerwick businesses, cruise passengers | "Know when the ships are in — and make the most of it" | Lerwick Port Authority, Living Lerwick, businesses, press, passenger‑facing SEO | Day‑views; "in port today" opens |

The sections that **work solo on day one** (What's On, Spik, Da Boats, Memories, the directory, **Cruise**) make the app useful *before* network effects — they carry the cold‑start while commerce (Fetch, Wallet, claimed listings, hubs) builds. Cruise is fully seeded already (a whole season — ~150 calls, ~140k passengers), so it's useful the moment someone opens it.

**Why Cruise punches above its weight:** it's three audiences in one feature, from one dataset.
- **Locals** get a **Barometer** (quiet → busy → very busy → peak) to plan around the crowded days — daily utility that builds habit.
- **Businesses** get a **footfall forecast**, which sharpens the Local/Directory claim pitch: *"see the cruise calendar — plan your staffing, stock and offers around the busy days."* A concrete reason to claim a page.
- **Cruise passengers** — ~140,000 high‑intent visitors physically stepping off in Lerwick each summer — get day‑ashore plans, what's on, and the shops and cafés near the berth. A captive visitor audience handed straight to local businesses.

## 4. Channels that actually work in Shetland (ranked)

1. **Word of mouth & community trust** — the #1 channel. Engineer it: ambassadors, hubs as multipliers, referral, gifting, "claim your business," visible local benefit.
2. **Facebook + Instagram** — where Shetland lives online. A genuine OneShetland presence posting daily Spik / weekly "What's On" / Memories. Work *with* group admins; never spam.
3. **Local press & radio** — *Shetland Times, Shetland News, BBC Radio Shetland, SIBC*. Coordinate a launch‑week hit across all four. Founder + community‑benefit story.
4. **Physical & in‑person** — shop‑window stickers + NFC tiles, posters on community noticeboards, a stall at shows/regattas/markets, presence around Mareel, the Co‑op, Toll Clock, ferry terminals (yes — even the Bressay ferry waiting room).
5. **Institutional partners** — Promote Shetland / shetland.org, Living Lerwick (BID), **Lerwick Port Authority** (the cruise schedule & a natural cruise‑day co‑promotion partner), SIC, COPE, charities, sports & community clubs, community councils.
6. **Email** — waitlist → launch → a weekly "Whit's on in Shetland" roundup (also cross‑posted to socials/press).
7. **Paid (minimal)** — lightly boosted FB posts geo‑targeted to Shetland; Google search for visitor‑intent terms ("Shetland events", "things to do in Shetland"). Small spend, high relevance — paid is the seasoning, not the meal.

## 5. 90‑day rollout

### Phase 0 — Pre‑launch (the 4–6 weeks before)
- **Make it alive on day one:** seed events, daily Spik, boats, and the directory (done — 134 businesses with logos). An empty app kills trust.
- **Recruit the supply side:** Fetch drivers (no drivers = no deliveries), 5–10 flagship **hubs** (clubs/charities — they bring members), and pre‑brief businesses ("your page is ready to claim").
- **Sign up local champions/ambassadors** — a few well‑kent folk across areas and interests (a knitter, a skipper, a club secretary, a shop owner).
- **Build a waitlist** on the web ("Coming soon — everything Shetland in one place").
- **Brief the press** under embargo: exclusive founder/community‑benefit angle to *Shetland Times*, *Shetland News*, *BBC Radio Shetland*, *SIBC*.
- **Tease** in FB groups via genuine, useful posts (a Spik word, a "what's on this weekend") — earn attention, don't blast it.

### Phase 1 — Launch week
- **Coordinated press & radio hit** across all four outlets in one week.
- **A launch moment** — a Lerwick pop‑up / market stall / tie‑in to an event on the calendar. Somewhere physical to "show it on a phone."
- **Business claim drive** — walk Commercial Street with window stickers + NFC tiles; partner Living Lerwick. "Your page is already live — claim it free."
- **Flagship hubs go live** with a real campaign (a charity fundraiser with Gift Aid = perfect launch story and reinforces community‑first).
- **Launch incentive** — small local‑wallet credit / intro cashback; a "cover‑the‑fee for charity" angle that doubles as messaging.
- **Switch on referral + gifting loops.**

### Phase 2 — First 90 days (habit + proof)
- **Weekly cadence:** the "Whit's on" roundup; rotating Spik/Memories/Da Boats content; **business success stories** (social proof).
- **Daily habit hooks:** Spik wird, Games streaks, What's On, live Da Boats — with tasteful, user‑controlled notifications.
- **Deepen supply:** more claimed businesses, more hubs, more drivers; first repeat transactions (tickets, Fetch, wallet, donations).
- **Start the diaspora/visitor lane** (Section 7).

---

## 6. Seasonal calendar (ride Shetland's year)
- **Jan–Mar — Up Helly Aa season:** event listings + ticketing; community energy is peak.
- **Spring — Folk Festival:** tickets + What's On showcase.
- **Summer — shows, regattas & cruise season:** the cruise calendar runs ~May–Oct (~150 calls, ~140k passengers). Push the **Cruise Barometer**, day‑ashore guides and "shop local on cruise days"; run stalls at shows; Fetch for busy households; lean into the visitor angle. **Cruise turns "summer footfall" from a vague idea into a dated, data‑backed campaign.**
- **Autumn — Wool Week:** huge for global knitters & visitors — events, directory (yarn/craft shops), Spik, web/SEO. A flagship growth window.
- **Christmas — shop local + gifting + Fetch:** gifting flows, local wallet, delivery.

---

## 7. Cold‑start playbook (chicken‑and‑egg, per section)
- **Fetch:** recruit & vet drivers *before* launch; start with a few core routes (Lerwick + nearby) rather than island‑wide.
- **Directory:** already seeded — convert with the **"claim your free page"** drive; businesses with no website get real value.
- **Hubs:** onboard institutions first; each hub brings its whole membership.
- **Tickets:** sign 2–3 anchor event organisers/venues pre‑launch so there's something to buy day one.
- **Wallet:** seed with a launch credit + a few anchor businesses accepting it.
- **Cruise:** already seeded with a full season (~150 calls, ~140k passengers) — solo‑useful from day one, no network effect needed. Use it as a *showcase* of how rich a OneShetland section can be when pitching businesses and partners.

---

## 8. Diaspora, visitors & cruise passengers (web/SEO — beyond the 23k)
The **web** app turns Shetland's global affinity *and* its physical visitor flow into reach and economic value:
- **SEO content** around "Shetland events", "things to do in Shetland", Wool Week, dialect (Spik), heritage (Memories), and the fleet (Da Boats) → visitors plan trips, buy tickets, find local businesses.
- **Cruise passengers — the biggest, most concrete visitor lane.** ~140,000 people a season step off in Lerwick with a few hours to spend. The web Cruise pages (arrival/departure times, day‑ashore plans, the Barometer, and the shops/cafés near the berth) plus SEO for "Lerwick cruise port", "things to do in Lerwick on a cruise day" and individual ship names turn that footfall into directory views, ticket sales and local spend. This is footfall that *arrives whether we market or not* — Cruise simply routes it to local businesses. Partner with **Lerwick Port Authority** and **Promote Shetland**.
- **Shareable cultural content** (daily Spik wird, Memories map, boat tracking) travels naturally on social and to the diaspora.
- **Partnerships** with Promote Shetland / shetland.org and the museum & archives amplify this.
*Result: the diaspora, independent tourists **and** cruise passengers all spend into the local economy through the same platform.*

---

## 9. Budget (lean, founder‑led)
This is a community play, not an ad‑spend play. The expensive currency is **time and trust**, not money.
- **£0–low:** press/radio (earned), FB/IG organic, ambassadors, hubs, claim drive, waitlist/email.
- **Modest:** window stickers/NFC tiles, posters, show stall fees, launch incentive (wallet credit), a simple printed one‑pager.
- **Optional small paid:** boosted FB posts (geo‑Shetland) + a little Google search for visitor terms.
Keep paid minimal and measure it; reinvest into incentives/content that compound.

## 10. KPIs & targets (realistic for ~23k)
- **Pre‑launch:** waitlist sign‑ups; drivers recruited; hubs onboarded; businesses pre‑committed to claim.
- **Launch (first weeks):** registrations (aim for ~1,000–2,000 early, i.e. a meaningful share of households), businesses claimed, first tickets/deliveries/donations/wallet top‑ups.
- **Retention:** D7 / D30, weekly‑active households, Spik/Games streaks, repeat transactions.
- **Cruise:** day‑views and "in port today" opens (local habit); cruise‑passenger web sessions on peak days; business footfall‑planning usage; uplift in directory views / offers redeemed on cruise days (the proof that Cruise drives local spend).
- **North star:** **weekly‑active households** + **value flowing through Shetland** (tickets sold, deliveries completed, donations raised, wallet spend, cruise‑day local spend) — because the mission is local benefit, not vanity installs.

---

## 11. Risks & Shetland‑specific sensitivities
- **"Outside/corporate" perception** → local voices, transparency, founder story, visible community benefit; let ambassadors and hubs front it.
- **FB group etiquette** → partner with admins, post genuinely useful content, lean on earned media rather than promo blasts.
- **Cold‑start** → seed every side pre‑launch; lead with solo‑useful sections.
- **Small market / saturation** → compete on depth & retention, expand via diaspora/visitors on the web.
- **Sustaining content** → automate (Spik daily, What's On aggregation) + community‑generated (hub notices, business offers) + a light editorial calendar.
- **Drivers & vetting** (Fetch) → recruit early; verify licence + goods‑in‑transit insurance.
- **Cruise / overtourism sensitivity** → some residents feel cruise days crowd the town. Frame Cruise as *helping locals navigate* the busy days (the Barometer) and helping *local* businesses benefit from visitors who are already here — community‑first, never tourism boosterism.

*One line to remember: in Shetland you don't buy reach — you earn trust, and the community carries it. Build something genuinely useful for the islands, let kent folk vouch for it, and open as many front doors as Shetland has communities.*
